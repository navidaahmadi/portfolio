Teaching Philosophy

My Philosophy: Relationships, Inclusion & Play

I support each child’s whole development—physical, cognitive, social‑emotional, communication, language, and literacy—through responsive relationships and rich, play‑based experiences.

Play‑based Inclusive Relationship‑focused Safety & Well‑being Family Partnerships

My Philosophy Statement

As an Early Childhood Educator, I believe it is my responsibility to support the growth and development of infants, toddlers, preschoolers, and school‑age children in all domains—physical, cognitive, social‑emotional, communication, language, and literacy. I am passionate about creating opportunities for children to learn through play and hands‑on experiences, as these methods allow me to observe their interests, model positive behavior, and adapt instruction to meet each child’s individual skills and abilities.

My goal is to build an inclusive and welcoming environment where children and families from all backgrounds feel respected, valued, and at home. I place great importance on building strong, trusting relationships with both children and their families. By fostering open and ongoing communication, I can work in partnership with families and other professionals to support each child’s unique needs, routines, and learning plans.

Inclusion is at the heart of my philosophy. I want every child in my care to feel safe, supported, and encouraged to explore at their own pace while achieving their individual goals. I strive to create a classroom where children collaborate, develop a love for learning, and gain confidence in themselves. My hope is that each child leaves the classroom each day with a sense of accomplishment, new knowledge, and a growing curiosity about the world around them—excitement that will inspire them to return the next day eager to learn more.

“Children learn from anything and everything they see. They learn wherever they are, not just in special learning places.”

— John Holt, Learning All the Time
